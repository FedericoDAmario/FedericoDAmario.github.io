Federico D'Amario

Researcher, Bank of England

Ph.D. in Economics from Sapienza University of Rome (2024). Research interests span econometrics, macroeconometrics, causal inference, machine learning, forecasting, and Bayesian econometrics. Current work focuses on the economic effects of macroprudential policy and on developing double/debiased machine learning methods for time series applications.

Co-investigator on the research project "Reverse Cross-Fitting: Double/Debiased Machine Learning for Time Series" at Sapienza University of Rome, and consultant researcher at the Research Office of Confindustria. Previously a PhD Intern at the Bank of England (2024–2025) and consultant at the OECD.
