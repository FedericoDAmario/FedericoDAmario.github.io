Federico D'Amario

Research Economist developing machine learning methodologies for nowcasting, causal inference, and policy evaluation. Research focuses on bridging traditional econometric theory with modern computational approaches to address complex policy evaluation and nowcasting challenges in high-dimensional data environments.

Specialization areas include double/debiased machine learning methods for causal inference, causal forest algorithms for heterogeneous treatment effects, and high-frequency econometric models that leverage alternative data sources for real-time macroeconomic analysis and policy evaluation.

Research objective centers on providing robust empirical methodologies that inform evidence-based policy making while advancing theoretical understanding of economic dynamics in increasingly complex data-rich environments through rigorous statistical and computational approaches.
